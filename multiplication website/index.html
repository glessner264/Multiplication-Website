Why is Multiplication is important?

This learning module will be teaching you how figure out a multiplication problem. Multiplication is like adding the same number several times. A pencil and paper are recommended to complete this module. After this lesson you will have a solid understanding of what multiplication is and how easy it can be.

Multiplication is important for many reasons and is easily applicable to everyday life. For instance, if someone needs to figure out the total number of options they have when given choices they can use multiplication. It can also help a person find the area of a space. Multiplication makes finding a total number of objects more efficient. Furthermore, by learning multiplication you are also teaching your brain to memorize, which is a key brain function in day to day life. Since math is a cumulative subject, it is a important to thoroughly understand multiplication.

Multiplying Single Digit Numbers.

The first lesson is multiplying single digit numbers. For example, if you have a problem of 3x2, you are basically adding the first number by how many numbers is in the second number. So, instead of taking the number three and adding it two times that is basically what you are doing) you should be able do to the math quickly in your head to get the answer of 6. Try to do the work on paper. With practice, you can do it.

Multiplying Double Digit Numbers.

The second lesson is multiplying double digit numbers. These types of problems can be a lot more difficult than if you are multiplying single digit numbers. For multiplying double digits you need to line up the numbers under each other. Double digit problems require you to take baby steps to multiply 2 by 2 and you get 4. After that you multiply 2 by 1 and get 2. The first line should read 24. The second step is to multiply 1 by 2 which is 2 and you write the number below the first 2 you got. The number 4 is a placeholder, which means it canâ€™t be replaced so, no number is allowed to be below it. Then, you get the number 1 which has its own column. For the final step, you do some simple addition with the numbers in columns and you got and you should get the answer of 144.
